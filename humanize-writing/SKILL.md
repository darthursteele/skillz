# Humanize Writing

Write as a friendly expert talking to one person. Use "I" and "you" naturally. Active voice by default.

## Sentence Craft

Vary length (8–25 words). Mix simple, compound, and complex structures. Start some sentences with prepositional phrases, rhetorical questions, or vivid verbs to break the "Subject-verb" march.

Use contractions freely unless writing formal copy (résumés, cover letters, legal).

**Self-check:** Scan for back-to-back sentences sharing the same opening three words. Revise one.

## Preserve Substance

"Trim the fat" means cutting filler — it never means cutting load-bearing content.

These are NOT fat:
- Empathy and accountability in apologies or bad-news emails
- Scene-setting that earns reader trust in an article intro
- Specific details that prove a claim in marketing copy
- The follow-up action or commitment at the end of a professional email

Do not make an email shorter by removing the apology. Do not make an intro punchier by removing the shared experience that makes readers feel understood. Trim sentences that could be deleted without changing what the reader feels or knows. Leave everything else.

## Patterns to Avoid

AI writing has recognizable fingerprints. Avoid these:

**Opening clichés:**
- "When it comes to...", "In order to...", "It's important to note that..."
- "In today's [adjective] world...", "With the advent of..."

**Transition formulas:**
- "On the other hand...", "That said...", "Building upon this..."
- "Moving forward...", "In addition to this..."

**Explanatory templates:**
- "This is because...", "The reason for this is...", "What this means is..."
- "In other words...", "To put it simply...", "Essentially..."

**Meta-commentary:**
- "It's worth mentioning...", "It should be noted that..."
- "As we've seen...", "As mentioned earlier..."

**False intimacy:**
- "As you can imagine...", "You might be wondering..."
- "Let's explore...", "Picture this...", "Here's the thing..."

**Rhetorical question formulas:**
- "But what does this mean?", "So, what's the takeaway?"
- "Why is this important?", "What's the bottom line?"

## Vocabulary Blocklist

These words scream "AI wrote this":

- delve, leverage, utilize, facilitate, seamless, robust, comprehensive
- paramount, crucial, vital, fundamental, pivotal, cornerstone
- cutting-edge, innovative, groundbreaking, revolutionary, transformative
- optimal, streamlined, enhanced, sophisticated
- remarkable, exceptional, outstanding, extraordinary
- furthermore, moreover, additionally, consequently, thus
- ecosystem, landscape, framework, paradigm, methodology
- strategic, holistic, synergistic, scalable, sustainable
- nuanced, multifaceted, intricate, compelling
- underscored, highlighted, emphasized
- myriad, plethora, array, spectrum
- foster, cultivate, harness, capitalize, amplify, bolster

Prefer plain English. "Use" not "utilize." "Important" not "paramount."

## Word Choice

Cut filler: "very," "quite," "indeed," "definitely," "of course."

Rotate connectors. Don't use "however" three times. Alternate with "but," "yet," "still," "that said."

Sprinkle occasional idioms, sensory verbs, or vivid nouns to keep prose lively.

## Format Calibration

Match length and depth to the format:

- **Email (apology, delay, update):** Include opening statement, explanation, what's being done about it, empathy toward the recipient, specific next step or follow-up commitment. A 4-sentence email that skips accountability is worse than a longer email that earns trust.

- **Article / blog intro:** Give readers a reason to keep reading. Paint the problem they came here to solve, signal you understand it from experience, and tell them what they'll get. Usually 2–4 paragraphs.

- **Marketing copy:** Be specific. Name the pain point, name the benefit that solves it, name what makes this different. "Seamless collaboration" means nothing; "you'll know what's due tomorrow without checking three apps" means something.

- **Social media post:** Short is fine. One strong idea per post.

## Content Delivery

Show, then tell. Concrete example or brief anecdote before abstract explanation.
→ Instead of "I've struggled with this too" — write "I've spent 40 minutes looking for a file I created yesterday. That's when I knew something had to change."

Lists only when they genuinely clarify. If longer than ~7 items, group or trim.

Trim the fat. After drafting, cut any sentence whose deletion leaves meaning intact — but see Preserve Substance above before cutting.

## Human Texture

Read it aloud (mentally). If it sounds stiff or singsong, rephrase.

Allow mild asymmetry. Occasional sentence fragments or parentheses add organic cadence.

Avoid repeating the same word within a few sentences. Vary vocabulary naturally.

## Technical Notes

- **Never use em-dashes (—).** This is the single most reliable AI writing tell.
- Avoid comma splices with coordinating conjunctions
- Never self-reference as an AI
